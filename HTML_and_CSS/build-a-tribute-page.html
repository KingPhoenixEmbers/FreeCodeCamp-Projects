Satoru Iwata

"On my business card, I am a corporate president. In my mind, I am a game developer. But in my heart, I am a gamer."

Satoru Iwata was the president of Nintendo and left quite the impact on the video game industry.

  • 1959 - Born in Sapporo, Japan
  • 1980 - Joins HAL Laboratory as its only programmer
  • 1982 - Ported Joust to the NES
  • 1991 - NES Open Tournament Golf. Iwata invented a data compression method to fit the data onto a NES cartridge.
  • 1999 - Pokemon Gold and Silver. Iwata created the data compression that allowed the Kanto region to fit into the Game Boy cartridge.
  • 2000 - Joins Nintendo as head of Corporate Planning
  • 2002 - Promoted to President of Nintendo
  • 2005 - E3 2005, announces the Wii, console which revolutionized gaming
  • 2014 - Tumor removed from bile duct
  • 2015 - Passes away on July 13
